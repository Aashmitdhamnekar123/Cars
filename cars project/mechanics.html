How are Cars made

INVENTION

1. Research market, including in-house and field investigations, to identify the role of this product and its components in the global portfolio; define separation from similar models sold by sister brands
2. Identify special features, advantages, and potential world, U.S., or segment firsts
3. Define competitive set, target customers; set curb-weight, fuel-economy, and performance goals
4. Competitive assessment
5. Powertrain selection
6. Budget, funding, pricing, investment considerations
7. Computer-aided-engineering (CAE) analysis
8. Customer, press, analyst clinics

DESIGN

1. Interior—sketches, theme selection, model build, continuous reviews
2. Exterior—same as above until design freeze
3. Exterior colors, interior-trim materials selection Wind-tunnel assessment of theme models
4. Concept creation for management presentation, potential auto-show use Additional CAE
5. Management and engineering reviews

ENGINEERING

1. Additional CAE
2. Customer clinics aimed at gathering current model feedback, suggestions for improvements
3. Research advanced technologies—engines, transmissions, motors, electronic controls, manufacturing techniques (painting, metal forming, plastic molding), and emerging trends
4. Package, layout studies
5. Body design and development for crashworthiness, weight, durability (in conjunction with CAE efforts)
6. Aerodynamic development
7. Design, development, tuning, validation (in-lab and on-road) of: a. Powertrain 9. Chassis
10. HVAC, infotainment, seats, lighting systems
11. Hot-, cold-, wet-weather tests
12. Crash tests
13. Fuel-economy evaluations
14. Design for manufacturing and assembly studies
15. Component and manufacturing cost analysis
16. Collaborate with suppliers for R&D of purchased components
17. Safety and emissions certification
